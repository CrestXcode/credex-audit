# User Interviews

Three conversations conducted between May 21-25, 2026 to validate the product concept and inform design decisions.

---

## Interview 1 — I.S., Student, Personal Projects

**Format:** WhatsApp call, ~15 minutes
**Profile:** CS student, uses AI tools for personal projects, no paid subscriptions

### Notes

Tools currently used: Claude, ChatGPT, KiloAI, Blackbox — all free tiers.

**Direct quotes:**
- "ChatGPT Pro gave no limits, the answers were optimised and most importantly it was time saving"
- "Normal ChatGPT was not good technically so I shifted to Claude — it's straightforward and organised"
- "As a student we need to keep up with calculations too"
- "We value free tools and if they are worth it then why not sharing them"
- "Data is not much confidential that can't be shared"

**Most surprising thing:** Her number one ranked tool was KiloAI — not Claude or ChatGPT. This was completely unexpected and revealed that the AI tool landscape is broader than the mainstream tools. Users have strong opinions about niche tools that aren't on anyone's radar.

**What it changed:** Added a "Don't see your tool? We're adding more soon" note to the form so users with niche tools don't feel excluded and bounce.

---

## Interview 2 — S.D., Indie Hacker, Solo Builder

**Format:** LinkedIn DM conversation, ~20 minutes async
**Profile:** Solo builder working on side projects, uses AI tools daily

### Notes

Tools currently used: ChatGPT and Claude, both free tiers.

**Direct quotes:**
- "Mostly ChatGPT and Claude"
- "If I have to pay I will pay for ChatGPT as it offers more tokens"
- "I don't like complete vibe coding"
- "For ChatGPT Plus I think no" (on feeling overpaid)
- "I think for my case I won't be thinking much about the wasting case"

**Most surprising thing:** He chose ChatGPT over Claude specifically for **token limits**, not features or brand preference. Price wasn't the deciding factor — capability ceiling was. This reframes how the audit should present recommendations: not just "cheaper" but "same capability ceiling for less. Complete lack of cost awareness — not because he's careless, but because at solo level the pain point simply doesn't exist. The real target user is NOT the solo indie hacker — it's the engineering manager at a 5-10 person startup paying multi-seat invoices."

**What it changed:** Token limit context added to ChatGPT recommendations in audit engine reasoning.

---

## Interview 3 — D.C., Indie Hacker, Solo Builder

**Format:** LinkedIn DM conversation, ~10 minutes async
**Profile:** Solo builder, uses free AI tools, cost-conscious

### Notes

Tools currently used: ChatGPT and Claude, free tiers only.

**Direct quotes:**
- "No no, I'm using free one"

**Most surprising thing:** The conversation ended abruptly after revealing free tool usage — which itself is data. The user disengaged the moment cost became the topic, suggesting the pain point genuinely doesn't register for solo free-tier users.

**What it changed:** Significantly sharpened the GTM targeting. Stopped thinking about solo builders as primary users. Focused the product positioning on team leads and founders with multiple seats.

---

## Key Takeaways Across All Three

1. Free users don't feel the pain — the product is for teams paying multi-seat plans
2. Token limits matter as much as price when users choose tools
3. Niche tools exist that the mainstream doesn't track — the audit tool needs a "suggest a tool" escape hatch
4. Trust is not a major barrier — users are already sharing data everywhere
5. Shareability is high — all three said they'd share a useful free tool
